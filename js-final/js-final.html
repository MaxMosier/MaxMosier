Project Description:

While brainstorming ideas for this project, I was inspired by this video to make some sort of particle-like simulation program. To make it interactive, I decided it would be fun to have the user control the general motions of the "entities," as I call them, using their mouse! Going off of pretty much just that idea, I developed what I have now. Along the way, I made a lot of subjective decisions about certain variables such as the weights of influences of entities on each other, and in part out of being annoyed by having to constantly go and adjust the code to test out different weights, I eventually decided to give the power to control such variables to the user. This, I feel, makes for a slightly more fun page, as well. I could have included a lot more options, but didn't want to overcomplicate things, or do anything that would require a long description, and so settled on a few variables I felt were the easiest to understand.

How to Use:

On a computer, click and drag your mouse to leave behind a trail the "entities" will follow! By default, there is a visible trail you leave behind (which as you can see slowly fades away). You can click the "Show Drawn Paths" button to toggle this feature.

When entities get near one another, they begin turning to match the direction of others around them. By default, this happens quite slowly. The "Entity-Entity Influence" slider controls this influence entities have on each others' direction. When at 100% strength, this will result in a lot of clustering, while a strength of 0%, the entities will simply move around loosely.
